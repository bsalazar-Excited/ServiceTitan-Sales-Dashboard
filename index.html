Three levers: (1) Push Works over Essentials — every Essentials deal costs ~5-20/tech/month. Make the Works ask before pricing reveal. Frame onboarding as one-time vs monthly compounding value. (2) Close at 2-year terms — ramp pricing protects ARPU at month 18 at 99/tech. 1-year discounts pull below target. (3) Quantify ROI before pricing — deals where dollar ROI is stated before pricing average 93/tech. Without it: 71. Discount differently (ramp, free months, OB reduction) rather than per-tech cuts. Source: Comm+ coaching · ARPU analysis
💬 How do I compete against BuildOps?›
BuildOps is primarily a construction-focused platform. Where ST wins: (1) Our service + construction hybrid is stronger for shops doing both — BO tends to over-index on construction and under-deliver on service workflows. (2) Titan Intelligence and AI features are ahead of BO. (3) Onboarding and support reputation — BO churn is a real signal, multiple Slack threads confirm shops leaving BO for ST due to broken promises and poor onboarding. Frame it: "BO is great if you're 100% construction. If you're running any service side, we're built for that entire lifecycle." Source: #competitive Slack · TriplePoint MEP win notes
💬 Does ServiceTitan integrate with Sage?›
Sage Intacct: integration exists through partner-enabled accounting integrations. Sage 50: no native integration as of recent Slack threads (#mindcloud-servicetitan confirmed this). If the prospect is on Sage, flag it in SFDC and route through #ask-partner-enabled-accounting-integrations in Slack for the most current status. Don't present as a blocker — most commercial shops move their accounting to QBO as part of the ST onboarding and it's a positive change. Source: Confluence — Accounting Integrations FAQ · #mindcloud-servicetitan
💬 Does ST have AI outbound calls / voice agents?›
Yes — this is new and actively being developed. AI Voice Agents for call booking is a live feature. Confirm current capabilities at servicetitan.com/features/ai and the webinar recap at servicetitan.com/blog/webinar-recap-ai-voice-agents-call-booking before presenting to a prospect. If a prospect says they heard we didn't have this — that was true 1-2 years ago, not today. Loop in an SE for demo if the prospect is specifically interested. Source: #sales Slack · servicetitan.com/features/ai
💬 Can construction GMV pricing scale down if revenue drops?›
This is a real objection that comes up in construction accounts. GMV-based pricing is annual revenue based — if a shop's construction revenue drops year over year the pricing should adjust at renewal. The concern to address is that it's not locked in forever at peak revenue. Flag specific scenarios to your SE or deal desk for guidance. For the prospect conversation, emphasize that most shops' construction revenue grows year over year once they have ST optimizing it — the worry is usually theoretical. Source: #commercial-construction Slack
💬 Does ServiceTitan integrate with Corrigo?›
No native Corrigo integration currently. Note in SFDC and flag for product. Don't present as a blocker — position as roadmap. Common in commercial HVAC and garage door. Source: Confluence — Accounting Integrations FAQ · #ask-sales-and-estimates
💬 Can I book a non-ICP into CP for a pricing call?›
Yes — use the non-ICP dropdown in CP. Let the prospect know it creates time and space for pricing even if they don't need the full demo. Good use of this: construction-heavy accounts that are off-books but are genuinely interested in pricing. Source: #sales Slack
💬 What's the re-route threshold for Construction?›
Re-route if construction revenue is over 30% of total annual revenue. If 31-99%: ask how many of every 5 projects last 3+ months AND how many subcontractors per project. If 3+ projects over 3 months AND 4+ subcontractors → DQ. Must capture total annual construction revenue if 31-99%. Source: Confluence — ICP Talk Tracks
💬 How do I handle a prospect also looking at FieldEdge?›
600+ shops have switched from FE to ST. FE is residential-first. ST wins: (1) commercial job costing depth, (2) Works inventory, (3) construction module for 31-99% shops, (4) mobile offline on job sites, (5) no office staff ceiling. Frame before they go in: "Here's what to compare when you talk to them next week." Source: Confluence — Comm+ Hub
💬 What's the call review requirement this week?›
Week 2 (ending 5/15): 2 snippets showing ROI with business implication and cost allocation. Week 3 (ending 5/22): Review an HCP call, include urgency or clear commitment moment. Tracker due 9pm PST Friday. Completion required — AEs who miss get called out at Monday huddle. Source: Confluence — Commercial Team Call Reviews
💬 What's the minimum tech count to sell?›
Minimum 4 sellable licenses with at least 1 Managed Tech. All full-time (35+ hrs/week). In Commercial Construction: at least 1 crossover tech working 20%+ service jobs. DQ if fewer than 4. Source: Confluence — ICP Talk Tracks
💬 How does performance pay / commission for techs work in ST?›
Configurable payroll is included in the Works package. You can set commission thresholds per technician — if they sell above a certain amount or hit productivity targets, they get a bonus tier. Very flexible. This is one of the biggest wins for shops trying to incentivize their field team. Set up in the compensation module. Only available in Works, not Essentials — use this as a Works upgrade reason. Source: Comm+ product knowledge
💬 When is the next construction cohort onboarding slot?›
Check the live onboarding calendar in your deal desk or ask your SE. As of May 14: May 25 construction cohort slot available — next slot is June 29. Slots fill fast. Use this as urgency: "The May 25 cohort gets you live by early July. If we move today I can lock that for you." Always confirm current availability before presenting dates. Source: ST onboarding calendar · Comm+ Hub
💬 How does the QuickBooks data migration work?›
Initial pull from QBO at onboarding start. Rep keeps using QB until ST is fully built, then final transfer at go-live. QBO stays as the accounting/GL platform — everything above the line moves to ST. QBO sync included. After final pull, any new data entered in QB after that date has to be manually entered in ST. Set clear expectations with the customer about the final pull date. Source: Confluence — Accounting Integrations FAQ · #ac-distribution_pod
💬 When should I send the referral email?›
Week 3 of onboarding — customer is engaged and seeing value but not yet fully independent. Short, personal, ask for one warm intro. This dashboard auto-drafts at week 3 using Snowflake OB date + SFDC contact info. One tap to send. Never use a template that sounds like a template. Source: Comm+ team best practice
